# Article-Dscriptors


The results presented in this section were obtained from the integrated application of the methodological steps described previously. This approach allowed the construction of a consistent processing chain, from the initial binary image to the detailed numerical quantification of each grain. Initially, the watershed segmentation and separation process ensured the adequate individualization of particles, even in situations of contact between adjacent grains. The extraction of contours and the determination of the center of mass enabled the polar representation $r(\theta)$, a fundamental condition for the application of Fourier descriptors. From this parameterization, each contour was decomposed into a harmonic series, allowing the separate analysis of the geometric contributions associated with different spatial scales. With the reconstructed contours and the coefficients properly normalized, the roughness coefficients were then calculated in different spectral ranges, as well as conventional geometric descriptors such as roundness, circularity, and regularity.
